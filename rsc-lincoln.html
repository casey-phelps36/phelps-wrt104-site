Rock Spot Climbing: Lincoln

Image

The next gym on my climbing adventure was Rock Spot Climbing: Lincoln. The gym is in kind of an awkward spot, it’s halfway up a hill, so I think they did a good job utilizing the space. For parking, there are two handicap spots right out front, a couple more regular spots, and a larger lot further up the hill that you can get up and down using the stairs next to the building. They have a sign on the door explaining the parking situation which I may have needed if I hadn’t been able to snag a spot in front, but that map might be more useful online.

There are two bouldering sections in this gym, a few auto belay stations, and top rope sections. It felt like the main focus of this gym was the rope climbing areas, and the boulders were a secondary focus. That is not to say that I did not have fun with the boulders though; the routes were creative and enjoyable, and they maximize the minimal bouldering space available. The main boulder arch is right next to the doors, and this is where I spent the majority of my time. This wall is mostly overhang, and there was a great range of difficulty in the route setting. The second boulder section is mixed in with the lead climbing cave, which is something I have seen in a lot of gyms. How this works is the boulders and lead climbs start in the same spot, but the boulder problems have finish holds marked at a certain height while the lead routes for the same problem keep going up. I did not see anyone lead climbing the day that I went, and I’m not lead certified yet so I couldn't try them out myself. There were some little kids on the auto belays and they were having a blast, and a couple probably around my age was climbing near me on some of the top ropes. I did not want to pay extra to have a staff belay me on the top ropes, but from the ground the routes looked fun and the couple climbing was saying that there are some challenging climbs throughout the gym.

Image

Out of the three Rock Spot gyms in Rhode Island, I would rank this second. You cannot access it if there is a birthday party going on upstairs, which there was for most of my visit, but they have a good amount of dumbbells, three cardio machines, some yoga balls, medicine balls, pull-up bars, hang boards, and what seemed like a bouldering technique training wall.

There is a gear shop by the front desk, and Rock Spot Climbing members get a discount on anything there. They have shoes, apparel, chalk, and some other items a climber may need. The membership discount is a great perk because climbing gear is expensive. There was a poster in the bathroom and by the front door advertising that at a few of the local restaurants Rock Spot members can get 15% off. This is such a fun way to get people to eat at places in their community and support small businesses.

This gym has its charms, but it was not my favorite. If I was more of a top roper instead of a boulderer I may have enjoyed it more, but as a boulderer I felt like this was not the gym for me. As creative as the routes were, there were not as many options compared to other gyms.
