Central Rock Gym

The last gym I went to is the only gym that is not owned by Rock Spot Climbing. Central Rock Gym has locations all across the country, and one of them is in Warwick. The only thing that kept me from getting a membership here was the location, it takes at least 30 minutes for me to get there from campus or my house. Besides that, this was my favorite gym out of the four I visited.

They have a room dedicated to strength training, and I was very impressed with it. They have squat racks, benches, bands, dumbbells, cardio equipment, some other random pieces of equipment, a campus board, a tension board, and a spray wall. A tension board is similar to the moon and kilter boards that the other gyms have, and a spray wall is another endurance wall that just has as many different holds as they can fit on. It looked nicer than some of the gyms I have seen in high schools. There is also a room dedicated to yoga, which is not something that I usually do but I know a lot of climbers that love it. For people looking for more than just a climbing gym, this is the place.

In the climbing section, they have lead climbing, top rope, a couple of auto belays, and a lot of different bouldering sections. The lead climbing and top rope wall goes through the middle of the gym, and while watching other people climb it looked like there were options for all skill levels. There were only two auto belays that I saw in this section, and then a smaller auto belay area with smaller walls that looked like it was for birthday parties. I had a lot of fun bouldering here. They have specific sections for overhang, slab, and cave. The routes might be graded a bit higher than they actually are, but I know that may be subjective and someone else could disagree with me. I bounced around from wall to wall when I got tired of a certain style and always found something I wanted to do.

Besides the location, the only other gripe I have with this location is that it does feel like a chain. The Rock Spot Climbing gyms all have a local, almost cozy feel, while Central Rock feels like you are in a modern climbing chain. I have no problem with these modern gyms, they are typically the ones that have more space and bigger gyms, but the vibes are always a little better in a more homey gym.

Climbing Vocabulary

  • Bouldering - climbing on shorter walls without using a rope
  • Top roping - the climber attaches to a rope that is already anchored at the top of the climb before going up
  • Lead climbing - the climber and the rope start from the ground up, and the climber attaches the rope to anchors as they go up
  • Auto belay - a device that lets climbers top rope without the need of a belayer because the device manages the rope instead
  • Project - a climb is typically outside of a climbers current grade range that they are working towards completing
  • Dyno - a climbing move that where to climber jumps to the next hold with either one hand or both hands off the wall
  • Campus - climbing without using your feet
  • Belaying - managing the rope for a climber, taking in or feeding out slack as needed
  • Grading/Ratings - numerical, letter, or color values assigned to a climb to show its relative difficulty
  • Slab - a climbing wall that has a slightly negative angle so the wall tilts away from you, and the routes require more balance, precise footwork, and static movements
  • Overhang - a climbing wall that is more than a few degrees steeper than vertical, and require more strength to stay on the wall
  • Vert - a wall that is straight up and down
  • Problem/Route/Climb - all terms used for the path that someone is climbing
